Mentoring

I have been fortunate to train students and postdoctoral scholars whose work now shapes education policy, economics, public policy, and applied research. They have built careers in universities, government, philanthropy, research organizations, and policy institutions. I am proud of their work and grateful for what I have learned from them.

Many became collaborators whose work appears throughout the Research and Policy Work pages.

Building Mentoring Communities

First-generation students

As a first-generation college student, I have worked to support students navigating college without the inherited knowledge and networks that many classmates bring with them. At Harvard, I have mentored students through the Harvard First Generation Program.

Women in economics

I have worked with women economists through the Committee on the Status of Women in the Economics Profession and the Women in Economics Network, Australia. These networks build community through mentoring, research, awards, events, and professional support.

Interdisciplinary education research

At the University of Michigan, I founded the Institute of Education Sciences Postdoctoral Training Program in Education Policy, which trained postdoctoral scholars in applied education research and connected them to an interdisciplinary community of faculty, students, and policy partners.

Causal inference in education research

In 2011, I founded the Causal Inference in Education Research Seminar, an interdisciplinary workshop for work in progress. The seminar brought together researchers from economics, education, public policy, sociology, statistics, and related fields to strengthen causal research on education.
