From research to legislation

In a 2006 paper with Judith Scott-Clayton, Dynarski showed that just four questions could explain 90 percent of the variation in financial aid eligibility, and that the FAFSA contained more questions than the federal tax return. Senator Lamar Alexander, citing this research, made the case in a 2014 New York Times op-ed to reduce the application to a postcard-sized form. The FAFSA Simplification Act was signed into law in December 2020; beginning in 2023, the FAFSA contained 33 questions, down from 108, with direct IRS data transfer. Ford School coverage: Dynarski’s research and advocacy for a simplified FAFSA becomes reality, Translating research to policy (2018), NYT Editorial Board, "Time to fix the FAFSA" (2015), FAFSA postcard proposal gaining traction (2015), Fulfilling the Commitment report (2009). Global Education Evidence Advisory Panel

Appointed by the UK Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office, Dynarski is one of 12 international researchers on the Global Education Evidence Advisory Panel. The panel synthesizes global evidence on cost-effective approaches to improving learning in low- and middle-income countries, advising governments and development organizations on education investments. The panel issues regular reports on smart buys in education; the most recent is below.
